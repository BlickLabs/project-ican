Cannabis Information Center (CIC)

The Cannabis Information Center is a scientific and technical advisory body that provides information in an objective and timely manner, establishing an optimal strategy for particular information needs, under the direction of qualified professional, whose specific objective is: To respond to the demand of information in an objective way evaluated, updated and timely.

Functions

Functions

In general, there are two functions:

  • Active information: activities in which initiative is taken when making information, with an educational, training, research and dissemination orientation (it is mainly done through newsletters).
  • Passive information: activities that attends to the information demands with respect to particular cases (they are the users' consultations).

Specific activities

Provide information to health professionals.
Provide information to officials of state agencies aimed at optimizing cannabis decision-making.
Provide information to patients and the general public.

Who are our users?

Health Professionals: Doctors, pharmacists, oncologists, pediatricians, etc.
Researchers.
Students.
General Population

Information

What information do we provide?

  • Information on efficacy and safety.
  • Drug and food interaction.
  • Mechanism of action when used for scientific and medicinal purposes.
  • Availability in the national and international market.
  • Technological, toxicological and legal information; amongst other.
